## Chapter 15: The Experimental Program

A framework that cannot be tested is not a scientific theory. More importantly, a framework whose predictions are also predicted by its competitors is not doing distinctive work.

### Discriminative Predictions

These are predictions that NFT makes and its competitors do not:

**1. Radical pair isotope effects on consciousness thresholds.** Extending the Zadeh-Haghighi et al. finding, NFT predicts that isotope substitutions affecting nuclear spin in key radical pair sites should systematically alter consciousness-related measurements — anesthetic sensitivity, EEG complexity measures, or behavioral markers of conscious processing. No classical theory predicts that nuclear spin should affect consciousness [B].

**2. The AI discrimination test.** Build a Level 3 quantum neuromorphic substrate operating at criticality, then emulate Level 2 on the same hardware by decohering the quantum degrees of freedom. The architecture, connectivity, and criticality parameters remain identical. The only variable toggled is quantum indeterminacy. If Level 2 and Level 3 are indistinguishable, NFT fails [B].

**3. ENAQT optimum in radical pair dynamics.** If you could experimentally tune dephasing rates in the radical pair substrate, there should be a non-monotonic relationship between dephasing and navigational performance. Too little dephasing should impair performance (quantum localization). Too much should impair it (classical diffusion). There should be a sweet spot corresponding to the physiological operating point [B].

**4. Entanglement witness in radical pair systems.** Entanglement witness protocols applied to radical pair spin states in microtubule-associated proteins should yield statistics incompatible with any separable model under physiological conditions. If no entanglement is measurable, Level B is substantially weakened [B].

**5. Anti-Zeno spectral signature.** The microtubule substrate should exhibit spectral signatures characteristic of anti-Zeno dynamics — measurement-accelerated rather than measurement-frozen evolution — determined by the spectral density of the radical pair environment. No classical criticality model predicts a specific spectral boundary between Zeno and anti-Zeno dynamics in neural tissue [B].

**6. Back-action evasion zombie.** Eliminating quantum back-action on microtubule conformational states while preserving classical neural signaling should eliminate phenomenal experience while functional behavior continues. No competing theory predicts this [B].

### Predictions Already Tested

Honesty requires reporting the ledger.

**Topological disruption order: NOT SUPPORTED.** NFT predicted topological complexity declines before classical neural activity during propofol anesthesia. The TDA reanalysis showed the opposite. See Chapter 13 for full discussion.

**Excitonic ENAQT: MECHANISM RULED OUT.** The original proposed quantum mechanism — excitonic transport through tryptophan chromophore networks — was computationally falsified. The 0.18% quantum advantage at physiological temperature is negligible. The revised mechanism (radical pair spin coherence) emerged from this failure. See Chapter 8.

These negative results weaken specific predictions while leaving the broader framework intact. The topological disruption prediction needs refinement or replacement. The excitonic mechanism was replaced by a stronger one. Both results demonstrate that NFT responds to evidence rather than accommodating around it.

### Supportive Predictions

These are consistent with NFT but also consistent with competitors — they confirm the framework without distinguishing it:

- Geodesic neural trajectories during conscious processing (also predicted by dynamical systems theories)
- Topological complexity tracking consciousness level (also predicted by structural accounts)
- Discrete perceptual cycles at 40-80 Hz (also predicted by classical oscillatory binding)
- Quantum cognition signatures in decision-making (also predicted by quantum probability models without quantum substrate)

### What Would Kill the Theory

NFT specifies the conditions of its own death:

- If radical pair spin coherence in microtubules is conclusively too weak or too short-lived for any measurable effect at the network level → Level B dies
- If Level 2 and Level 3 systems are indistinguishable on all behavioral and informational measures → Level B dies
- If the 6D simulation advantage disappears when the maze structure is varied systematically → the proof of concept is an artifact
- If classical systems at criticality exhibit all five diagnostic markers (Chapter 16) → the entire quantum commitment dies
- If no isotope effects on consciousness are found in properly controlled experiments → the radical pair mechanism loses its empirical anchor

A theory that tells you how it can be killed is doing science. A theory that hides its failures is doing something else. This one reports both.
