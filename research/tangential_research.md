# Tangential Research: Papers Relevant to "Consciousness as Navigational Faculty"
## Compiled March 10, 2026

This report surveys academic work tangential to Ken Malloy's framework, which proposes that consciousness is a faculty — like vision or proprioception — that evolved to navigate the fourth dimension understood as the total possibility space of all 3D realities across all time. The framework positions IIT, GNWT, and Orch OR as describing subsystems of the same vehicle rather than competing theories, and invokes a Gödelian argument about the limits of describing consciousness from within.

Papers are organized by how they intersect with the framework's key claims.

## 1. DIMENSIONAL APPROACHES TO CONSCIOUSNESS

### Bernard Carr — "Making Space and Time for Consciousness in Physics"
**Source:** [PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/rec/CARMSA-10) | [Nova Science Publishers](https://novapublishers.com/shop/chapter-7-making-space-and-time-for-consciousness-in-physics/)

Carr (Emeritus Professor of Mathematics and Astronomy, Queen Mary University of London) proposes a **5-dimensional psychophysical space** that merges phenomenal (perceptual) space with physical space. The 5th dimension is associated with "mental time" — distinct from physical time. He argues that the "specious present" (the minimum timescale of conscious experience) may correspond to a compactified extra dimension, and that there exists a **hierarchy of levels of consciousness associated with a hierarchy of time dimensions**.

**Relevance to Ken's framework:** Carr is working the same dimensional intuition — consciousness requires extra dimensions beyond spacetime to be described. However, Carr adds dimensions to accommodate consciousness, while Ken's framework reinterprets the existing 4th dimension (time as possibility space) and makes consciousness the faculty that navigates it. These are complementary but distinct moves. Carr gives you academic cover for the dimensional approach without requiring you to add speculative new dimensions.

## 6. THE QUANTUM ZENO PROBLEM AND ITS RESOLUTION

### The Objection

The quantum Zeno effect (QZE) is the most significant formal objection to any consciousness-causes-collapse framework. Under QZE, continuous measurement of a quantum system prevents it from evolving — frequent observation locks the system into its current eigenstate. If consciousness continuously collapses the wave function, reality should freeze. Chalmers & McQueen (2021) identified this as falsifying naive consciousness-collapse models and introduced additional constraints (Φ-dependent collapse operators) to work around it.

### Resolution Within the Navigational Framework

The navigational framework resolves the Zeno problem without additional constraints. Continuous conscious collapse does not freeze reality — it is precisely what *produces stable, coherent experienced reality*. The Zeno effect is not a bug but the mechanism by which navigation maintains a coherent trajectory through possibility space. Objects appear solid, the world appears consistent, because consciousness is continuously selecting a coherent path. The "freezing" is *experienced as persistence* — the stability of the classical world.

Change and temporal flow arise because navigation is not static observation but active steering. Each collapse event is a new selection. The continuity of collapse produces the continuity of experience — analogous to a film projector where discrete frames produce the appearance of smooth motion.

### The Anti-Zeno Effect

Critically, the quantum anti-Zeno effect (AZE), discovered by Kaulakys & Gontis (1997) and experimentally confirmed, shows that under certain coupling regimes, frequent measurement *accelerates* rather than freezes quantum transitions. Whether a system exhibits Zeno (freezing) or anti-Zeno (acceleration) behavior depends on the relationship between the measurement rate and the spectral density of the environment. This means continuous conscious collapse could drive quantum evolution forward depending on the spectral properties of the neural substrate.

### Supporting Evidence: Discrete Perceptual Cycles

Hameroff et al. (2025) propose that Orch OR collapse events in microtubules account for discrete perceptual cycles at approximately 40-80 Hz — each frame a collapse/selection event. Conscious active inference models (Safron et al., 2025) integrate this with predictive processing, showing how rapid discrete collapse events produce subjectively continuous experience.

## 7. THE IWMT COUNTERPUNCH AND ITS FAILURE

### The Strongest Objection From IWMT

Integrated World Modeling Theory (Safron, 2020/2022) presents the most sophisticated competing unification. It combines IIT + GNWT + Free Energy Principle + Active Inference under a single mathematical formalism (variational free energy minimization). Its key advantages over the navigational framework: (1) a single principle that *derives* why integration, broadcasting, and embodiment are needed rather than asserting them as subsystems, (2) testable predictions about neural dynamics grounded in existing Bayesian inference frameworks, and (3) no dependency on controversial quantum consciousness claims.

IWMT's strongest critique of the navigational framework: "navigation" is a metaphor without formal mathematical content. Until navigation can be written as a formal operation, the framework is poetic rather than scientific.

### Where IWMT Breaks

Two observations expose fatal gaps in IWMT:

**1. The AI Embodiment Problem.** IWMT claims consciousness arises when integrated information and global workspaces function as Bayesian belief networks for *embodied agents*. But large language models (e.g., Claude, GPT) arguably satisfy these formal criteria — they perform variational inference, build world models, minimize prediction error, and run on physical substrates. IWMT must either (a) accept that such systems are conscious (which contradicts strong intuition and the systems' own self-reports), or (b) add ad hoc constraints about what "embodiment" really means. The theory doesn't specify the threshold, making "embodiment" a hand-wave where a mechanism should be.

**2. Time as Furniture.** IWMT inherits the Free Energy Principle's treatment of time as the dimension along which predictions unfold — a background parameter, not an explanatory target. It works *in* time but doesn't account *for* time. It cannot explain why there is a subjective experience of temporal flow, why the arrow feels the way it does, or why there is a "now." The navigational framework treats temporal experience as the central phenomenon to be explained: time doesn't just happen to consciousness — consciousness *is* the navigation of the temporal/possibility dimension.

### Implications

These failures suggest IWMT describes the computational *implementation* of consciousness (how the brain processes information) but not its *nature* (what consciousness actually is and does). The navigational framework targets the nature; IWMT targets the implementation. They may ultimately be complementary rather than competing — IWMT describing the vehicle's engine, the navigational framework describing what driving is.

## 8. FORMALIZING NAVIGATION: CANDIDATE MATHEMATICAL FRAMEWORKS

### The Problem

"Navigation through possibility space" needs a precise formal definition. Without one, the framework remains a metaphor. Several existing mathematical frameworks are candidates for providing this formalization.

### Candidate 1: Riemannian Geometry — Consciousness as Geodesic Path Selection

**Lu, M. (2024). "A mathematical framework of intelligence and consciousness based on Riemannian Geometry." arXiv:2407.11024.**
https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11024

Core idea: Intelligence elements (tokens, concepts, percepts) are embedded in a high-dimensional space where their learned interconnections form manifolds. Sequential activation of these elements constitutes *thought flow*, which moves along manifold *geodesics* — the shortest paths through curved space. The direction of thought (the tangent vector of the geodesic) is determined by the manifold's intrinsic Riemannian geometry. Consciousness operates as the self-referential process that perceives the thought flow, evaluates it against predictions, and provides feedback through prediction errors, adjusting the geodesic path.

**Relevance to Ken's framework:** This is potentially the most natural formalization. Navigation through possibility space becomes *geodesic path selection on a Riemannian manifold*. The possibility space is the manifold. Consciousness is the process that selects which geodesic to follow. Collapse events correspond to points where the path is determined. The Riemannian metric encodes the "landscape" of possibility — which paths are nearby, which are distant, which are energetically favorable. Steering = adjusting the tangent vector at each point.

**Formal advantage:** Riemannian geometry is well-understood, computationally tractable, and already used in physics (general relativity) and neuroscience (state-space models of brain dynamics). It provides a natural language for talking about paths, distances, curvature, and geodesics — all of which map onto the navigational metaphor.

**Testable prediction:** If consciousness is geodesic navigation, then neural state trajectories during conscious perception should follow geodesic-like paths on the neural state manifold, while unconscious processing should show non-geodesic (less efficient, more meandering) trajectories. This is measurable with existing neuroimaging techniques.

### Candidate 2: Category Theory — Consciousness as Functor Between Domains

**Prentner, R. (2024). "Category theory in consciousness science: going beyond the correlational project." Synthese, 204(69).**
https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-024-04718-5

**Phillips, S. & Tsuchiya, N. (2024). "Towards a (meta-)mathematical theory of consciousness: universal (mapping) properties of experience." arXiv:2412.12179.**
https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.12179

Core idea (Prentner): Category theory provides tools for relating *different kinds* of mathematical structures through functors (structure-preserving maps between categories). Applied to consciousness, a functor maps between the category of physical states and the category of phenomenal states. The key insight: *adjoint functors* — paired functors that are "best approximations" of each other — may formalize the relationship between objective physical descriptions and subjective experience. This addresses the hard problem not by dissolving it but by formalizing the precise sense in which physical and phenomenal descriptions are related without being identical.

Core idea (Phillips & Tsuchiya): All six axioms of IIT can be derived from a single categorical notion — the *universal mapping property* (a unique-existence condition). Their slogan: "Consciousness is a universal property." This provides a meta-mathematical foundation that subsumes IIT's axioms under a single categorical principle.

**Relevance to Ken's framework:** Category theory could formalize the relationship between the three subsystems. IIT, GNWT, and Orch OR each describe categories of structures. The navigational framework posits specific functors between them — maps that preserve relevant structure while translating between integration (IIT), broadcasting (GNWT), and quantum selection (Orch OR). The claim that these are subsystems of one vehicle becomes: there exists a commutative diagram relating the three categories, and consciousness is the universal property that makes the diagram commute.

The adjoint functor approach to the hard problem is particularly promising. The subjective-objective gap isn't a bug — it's the inherent asymmetry between a functor and its adjoint. They're related, they constrain each other, but they're not identical. That's formally precise and philosophically satisfying.

**Formal advantage:** Category theory is the most abstract and general mathematical framework available. It's the "mathematics of mathematics." If consciousness is truly fundamental (as Ken's framework suggests), it may require this level of abstraction.

### Candidate 3: Quantum Decision Theory in Hilbert Space

**Helland (2021-2025). "A new foundation of quantum decision theory." arXiv:2310.12762.**
https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.12762

**Busemeyer & Bruza (2012, expanded 2023-2024). "Quantum Models of Cognition and Decision."**
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11854726/

Core idea: Cognitive states are represented as vectors in Hilbert space. Decisions correspond to projections onto subspaces — collapsing a superposition of possibilities into a definite outcome. The mathematics of quantum measurement (projection operators, Born rule probabilities) formalize how an agent selects from incompatible alternatives. Human decision-making empirically violates classical probability (conjunction fallacy, order effects) but follows quantum probability.

**Relevance to Ken's framework:** Navigation through possibility space becomes *projection operations in Hilbert space*. The possibility space is the full Hilbert space. Each conscious moment is a projection — collapsing the state vector onto a subspace corresponding to the selected reality. The Born rule gives the probability of selecting each possibility. This directly formalizes consciousness-as-collapse in the language of quantum mechanics.

**Formal advantage:** This is the most physically grounded option — it uses the actual mathematics of quantum mechanics rather than analogical extensions. If Orch OR is correct about quantum processes in microtubules, then navigation literally IS projection in Hilbert space.

**Key empirical support:** Human cognition already behaves according to quantum probability. This isn't metaphorical — the math works. Conjunction fallacies, order effects, and disjunction effects all follow from quantum formalism but violate classical probability.

### Synthesis: A Multi-Scale Formalism

These three frameworks may operate at different scales:

- **Hilbert space / quantum decision theory** — the *microscale*. The physics of individual collapse events in microtubules. Navigation at the quantum level.
- **Riemannian geometry** — the *mesoscale*. The dynamics of neural state trajectories. Navigation at the level of brain activity.
- **Category theory** — the *macroscale*. The structural relationships between different descriptions of consciousness. Navigation at the level of theory unification.

A complete formalization may require all three, related by functors (category theory) that map quantum-level descriptions (Hilbert space) to neural-level descriptions (Riemannian manifolds) in a structure-preserving way.

## 9. THE DELANCEY PROBLEM: KOLMOGOROV COMPLEXITY AND THE TEMPORAL CATEGORY ERROR

### DeLancey's Position

Craig DeLancey (SUNY Oswego) argues in *Consciousness as Complex Event: Towards a New Physicalism* (Routledge, 2023) that phenomenal experiences are mysterious only because they are Kolmogorov-incompressible — too descriptively complex for our theories to capture. The explanatory gap is epistemic (we lack sufficient information), not ontological (there's nothing non-physical going on). Consciousness is a complex physical event, full stop.

His three core claims: (1) paradigmatically mysterious phenomenal experiences have constitutive properties that are extremely complex, (2) our current theories and observations do not include all the information required to explain those experiences, and (3) our judgments and memories of experiences cannot include all the information required to explain them. From these, he derives that all canonical anti-physicalist arguments (knowledge, modal, conceivability, gap, zombie) fail because they would appear compelling whether consciousness is non-physical *or* simply very complex.

**Source:** DeLancey, C. (2023). *Consciousness as Complex Event: Towards a New Physicalism.* Routledge. https://www.routledge.com/Consciousness-as-Complex-Event-Towards-a-New-Physicalism/Delancey/p/book/9781032341316

### McQueen's Existing Critique

Kelvin McQueen (the same McQueen from Chalmers & McQueen 2021 on consciousness-collapse) reviewed DeLancey's book for Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews. Key criticisms: (1) DeLancey never actually estimates the Kolmogorov Complexity of any phenomenal experience or any theory of consciousness — the claims are unquantified, (2) the incompressibility cost principle he relies on is not proven, (3) Kolmogorov Complexity may not be the right complexity measure for these purposes.

**Source:** McQueen, K. (2023). Review of *Consciousness as Complex Event*. Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews. https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/consciousness-as-complex-event-towards-a-new-physicalism/

### The Temporal Absence

A thorough search of DeLancey's text reveals **zero substantive treatment of temporal experience**. The terms "temporal experience," "experience of time," "subjective time," "flow of time," "arrow of time," "specious present," and "passage of time" do not appear. "Entropy" appears once, in a citation to Bekenstein's physics paper on entropy-to-energy bounds. "Thermodynamic" does not appear.

DeLancey's framework treats phenomenal experiences as *events* — discrete, bounded occurrences. He never asks why experience has temporal flow, why it feels like it's happening *now*, or why there is an experienced arrow of time.

### The Double Jump Problem

DeLancey's Kolmogorov Complexity argument requires two explanatory jumps, and the second one is structurally impossible with his tools:

**Jump 1 (DeLancey's claim — qualia):** Phenomenal experience seems mysterious because it's Kolmogorov-incompressible. The explanatory gap is epistemic. *This is at least internally coherent, even if McQueen's critique shows it's unproven.*

**Jump 2 (required but absent — time):** The experience of temporal flow must also be explained. If consciousness is "just" a complex physical event, then the experience of flow is either:

- **(a) An illusion produced by the complex event.** But this is a buck-shift, not an explanation. It pushes the mystery from "why does this neural event produce the experience of redness?" to "why does this neural event produce the experience of temporal flow?" The hard problem hasn't been solved — it's been relocated one level down. This is the same move Dennett makes with the Cartesian theater, and it has been extensively criticized as explaining *away* the phenomenon rather than explaining it (Chalmers, 1995; Block, 2007).

- **(b) Something the complex event is *doing* — an active process.** But this starts requiring exactly the kind of navigational/functional account that DeLancey's framework doesn't provide.

### The Category Error: Kolmogorov Complexity Is Atemporal

The deeper problem is that Kolmogorov Complexity is the wrong mathematical tool for temporal phenomena, and this can be shown formally:

1. **Kolmogorov Complexity measures static objects.** It is defined as the length of the shortest program that produces a given string. The string is a completed, static object. The measure says nothing about *process* or *dynamics*.

2. **Kolmogorov Complexity is symmetric with respect to time.** The complexity of a string read forwards equals the complexity of the string read backwards, up to a constant (K(x) ≈ K(reverse(x))). There is no arrow in descriptive complexity.

3. **The Kolmogorov-Shannon equivalence is statistical, not physical.** For computable probability distributions, expected Kolmogorov Complexity equals Shannon entropy up to a constant (Grünwald & Vitányi, 2004). Shannon entropy maps to thermodynamic entropy via Boltzmann's constant. But this equivalence holds over *ensembles*, not individual objects — and Kolmogorov Complexity itself carries no thermodynamic content.

4. **Brudno's theorem** connects algorithmic complexity to entropy *rate* for dynamical systems, but only for *ergodic* processes. Consciousness is almost certainly non-ergodic — it does not uniformly sample its state space over time.

Therefore: Kolmogorov Complexity can tell you an experience is complex. It cannot tell you why it *moves*. It cannot explain the arrow of temporal experience. It cannot distinguish a frozen snapshot of maximal complexity from a dynamically evolving conscious process. DeLancey's central tool is structurally incapable of addressing temporal consciousness.

### The Double Jump's Incredibility

DeLancey needs complexity to do two things that pull in opposite directions:

- For **qualia** (Jump 1): complexity explains why experience *resists description* — it's too rich for our theories.
- For **time** (Jump 2): complexity would need to explain why experience *has intrinsic direction* — but Kolmogorov Complexity is direction-invariant.

The very tool he uses to dissolve the hard problem of qualia is formally incapable of addressing the hard problem of time. This is not a gap that can be waved away with "it's very complex." It's a category error — like using a ruler to measure temperature.

### The Buck-Shift Pattern

This pattern — dissolving one hard problem only to face a harder one at the next level — has been identified in consciousness studies before:

- Dennett's "explaining away" strategy dissolves qualia but cannot explain why the *explanation* itself feels like something (Chalmers, 1995).
- Functionalist accounts explain cognitive access but not phenomenal experience (Block, 1995 — the access/phenomenal distinction).
- Identity theory explains correlation but not why *this* physical process produces *this* experience (Levine, 1983 — the original explanatory gap).

DeLancey's Kolmogorov move is the most sophisticated version of this pattern, but it follows the same structure: dissolve the mystery at one level (qualia → it's just very complex), face the unreduced mystery at the next level (time → ???). Each iteration of the buck-shift adds incredibility because the remaining mystery is harder, not easier, than the one that was "dissolved."

### Implications for Ken's Framework

DeLancey's failure to account for time is not incidental — it's structural. Any framework that treats consciousness as a static property (complexity, information, integration) will face this problem. The navigational framework succeeds precisely where DeLancey fails because it treats temporal experience as the *primary* phenomenon to be explained, not a secondary feature to be derived.

The argument can be stated simply: **if you can't explain why consciousness moves through time, you haven't explained consciousness.**

## 10. AIT'S OWN EVOLUTION ARGUES AGAINST DELANCEY — AND THEN AGAINST ITSELF

### The Setup: AIT Outgrew Kolmogorov Complexity

Algorithmic Information Theory didn't stop at Kolmogorov Complexity. Its own internal development traces a path from static description toward dynamics, meaning, and temporality — each step an implicit acknowledgment that the original tool was insufficient:

**Stage 1: Kolmogorov Complexity (1960s)** — Length of shortest program producing a string. Static. Atemporal. Direction-invariant. *This is DeLancey's tool.*

**Stage 2: Bennett's Logical Depth (1988)** — The *computation time* required by the shortest program to produce its output. A string can be Kolmogorov-simple but logically deep — it required extensive computation to produce even though the program is short. Logical depth captures *process*: the history of computational work. A deep object "looks like it required a lot of effort to produce." Bennett explicitly introduced time into AIT because description length alone was insufficient for physical complexity.
- Bennett, C.H. (1988). "Logical Depth and Physical Complexity." In *The Universal Turing Machine: A Half-Century Survey*, pp. 227-257.

**Stage 3: Gell-Mann & Lloyd's Effective Complexity (1996)** — Length of compressed description of an object's *regularities only*, stripping out randomness. Separates meaningful structure from noise. Critically: effective complexity is *observer-relative* — what counts as regularity vs. randomness depends on the observer's epistemic context. Consciousness enters the measurement.
- Gell-Mann, M. & Lloyd, S. (1996). "Information measures, effective complexity, and total information." Complexity, 2(1), 44-52.

**Stage 4: Zenil's Algorithmic Information Dynamics (2017-2020)** — Combines AIT with *perturbation analysis* and *causal calculus*. Instead of "how complex is this object?", AID asks "how does complexity *change* when you intervene?" AIT made dynamic and causal. Explicitly applied to consciousness in *Neuroscience of Consciousness* (Oxford, 2017).
- Zenil, H. et al. (2017). "An algorithmic information theory of consciousness." Neuroscience of Consciousness, nix019.
- Zenil, H. et al. (2019). "An Algorithmic Information Calculus for Causal Discovery." iScience, 19, 1160-1172.

**Stage 5: Tsallis Entropy Derived from Chaitin-Kolmogorov (February 2026)** — Shows that imposing non-local restrictive grammar rules on universal Turing machine string generation yields *Tsallis entropy* instead of Shannon entropy. Algorithmic cost follows a *power-law* of string length instead of linear growth. This is AIT native to non-extensive, non-ergodic systems.
- arXiv:2602.03919 (Feb 2026). "Tsallis Entropy derived from the Chaitin-Kolmogorov Informational Entropy."

### The Argument That Helps Us

Each stage of AIT's evolution adds something Kolmogorov Complexity lacked:

| Stage | What's Added | What's Missing from KC |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Bennett (1988) | Computation *time* | KC ignores process |
| Gell-Mann (1996) | *Meaning* (regularity vs. noise) | KC treats noise and structure equally |
| Zenil (2017) | *Dynamics and causality* | KC is static |
| Tsallis-Chaitin (2026) | *Non-extensive, power-law scaling* | KC assumes linear/extensive regime |

DeLancey chose Stage 1. AIT itself moved to Stage 5. The field's own development is an implicit argument that static Kolmogorov Complexity is insufficient for describing complex physical systems — and the brain is the most complex physical system we know.

If DeLancey had used logical depth (Stage 2), he'd have a tool that acknowledges temporality. If he'd used effective complexity (Stage 3), he'd have observer-relativity. If he'd used AID (Stage 4), he'd have dynamics. If he'd used the Tsallis-Chaitin framework (Stage 5), he'd have the right entropy for the brain's actual operating regime.

**But he used none of these.** He used the simplest, most static tool in the AIT toolkit and declared consciousness explained.

### The Pivot: Now Drop All Of It

Here is where the argument turns.

Even if DeLancey had used the most advanced AIT tools — even if he'd used Tsallis-derived algorithmic complexity with logical depth and causal perturbation analysis — **it still wouldn't be enough**. Because AIT, at every stage, is a theory of *computation*. It is Turing machines all the way down. And Turing machines are:

1. **Deterministic** (given input and program, output is fixed)
2. **Classical** (no genuine quantum indeterminacy)
3. **Discrete** (operates on strings, not continuous dynamics)
4. **Substrate-independent** (the same computation on any hardware)

Consciousness, as argued in this framework, requires:

1. **Genuine indeterminacy** (quantum coupling to possibility space)
2. **Substrate dependence** (quantum coherence in biological microtubules — not any hardware)
3. **Continuous temporal embedding** (existence in entropy-producing, thermodynamically irreversible time)
4. **Non-computability** (Penrose's argument that consciousness involves non-algorithmic processes)

AIT — *all* of AIT, not just Kolmogorov Complexity — is a theory about what Turing machines can describe. If Penrose is right that consciousness involves non-computable processes, then no algorithmic information measure, however sophisticated, can capture it. You're measuring the shadow, not the object casting it.

The Tsallis-Chaitin paper (2026) is illuminating here: it shows that constraining the Turing machine's grammar (imposing non-local rules on string generation) produces Tsallis entropy. But the constraints are *imposed on the machine from outside*. The machine itself doesn't choose them. In Ken's framework, consciousness *is* the thing that imposes constraints on which possibilities are realized — it's the navigator, not the computation. AIT can describe the *output* of navigation (the selected path through possibility space) but not the *act* of navigation itself, because navigation requires the very thing Turing machines lack: genuine selection from quantum indeterminacy.

### The Neuroscience That Forces This Conclusion

The brain doesn't operate in the regime where standard AIT (or even Tsallis-extended AIT) cleanly applies. The empirical evidence:

**The brain operates at criticality — far from equilibrium:**
- States of unconsciousness (anesthesia, disorders of consciousness) are characterized by *departure from criticality* (Nature Communications Biology, 2024: "Critical dynamics in spontaneous EEG predict anesthetic-induced loss of consciousness")
- Self-organized criticality maximizes information capacity and dynamic range; entropy is high but not unbounded, producing rich metastable patterns (Neuron, 2025: "Is criticality a unified setpoint of brain function?")
- Degree of nonequilibrium is elevated during cognitive tasks and reduced in unconsciousness, sleep, and Alzheimer's disease (PNAS, 2025: "Multilevel irreversibility reveals higher-order organization of nonequilibrium interactions in human brain dynamics")

**The brain is non-ergodic and time-irreversible:**
- Brain dynamics show evidence of time-irreversibility and broken detailed balance — the brain operates in a *nonequilibrium stationary state* (Nature Scientific Reports, 2024: "The temporal asymmetry of cortical dynamics as a signature of brain states")
- Non-equilibrium whole-brain dynamics arise from pairwise interactions generating entropy production (Cell Reports Physical Science, 2025)
- Violations of the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem decrease in unconscious states vs. conscious conditions

**Tsallis and Rényi entropies already outperform Shannon in brain measurement:**
- Tsallis entropy successfully describes systems with long-range interactions, multifractal space-time constraints, and long-term memory effects — all characteristic of neural dynamics
- Rényi entropy-complexity causality space detects scale-free features in EEG/iEEG data more effectively than Shannon-based measures (Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience, 2024)
- Rényi permutation entropy performs best at tracking EEG changes during different anesthesia states
- Brain networks maintain scale-free organization across wakefulness, anesthesia, and recovery — this structure is captured by Rényi, not Shannon

**The Kolmogorov-Shannon equivalence breaks in the brain's regime:**
- The equivalence E[K(X)] = H(X) + O(1) holds for computable distributions and Shannon entropy only
- For Tsallis entropy: the relationship fails for certain distribution classes (Teixeira et al., 2011)
- For Rényi entropy: the equivalence also fails for non-Shannon parameter values (Teixeira et al., 2011)
- Since the brain's statistical structure is better described by Tsallis/Rényi, Kolmogorov Complexity's connection to the brain's actual information dynamics is *formally broken*

The argument has three moves:

**Move 1 (gentle):** DeLancey used the simplest tool in AIT. AIT's own evolution (Bennett → Gell-Mann → Zenil → Tsallis-Chaitin) shows this was insufficient. Each successor added something KC lacked: time, meaning, dynamics, non-extensivity.

**Move 3 (empirical):** The neuroscience confirms this. The brain operates at criticality, far from equilibrium, in a non-ergodic, time-irreversible regime. Its statistical structure is Tsallis/Rényi, not Shannon. The formal connection between Kolmogorov Complexity and the brain's actual information dynamics is broken. DeLancey brought a Shannon ruler to a Tsallis world.

## 11. PROPOSED MECHANISM: LONG-RANGE CORRELATIONS AND THE BINDING PROBLEM

### The Problem

The brain exhibits long-range temporal correlations (LRTCs), power-law neural avalanches, and Tsallis/Rényi statistical signatures — all hallmarks of a system with global coupling. Classical neuroscience attributes binding to gamma-band synchrony (30-100 Hz), but synchrony describes *what* binding looks like, not *how* it works. The mechanism question: what physical process produces the long-range correlations that bind distributed neural activity into unified conscious experience?

### The Standard Answers and Their Gaps

**Classical synchrony (Engel, Singer, Melloni):** Gamma oscillations correlate with conscious perception and propagate across distant brain regions (Melloni et al., 2007). But correlation isn't mechanism — what *produces* the synchrony? Axonal conduction delays are too slow and noisy for the precision observed.

**Orch OR's quantum binding (Hameroff et al., 2025):** Quantum coherence in microtubule networks solves the binding problem "in principle" — a collective quantum state across microtubules is inherently unified. Babcock et al. (2024) demonstrated superradiance in microtubule tryptophan networks at room temperature, with delocalized excitons spanning >10⁵ tryptophan sites. Mesoscopic coherent domains achieve 1-10 ms coherence times — matching the timescale of conscious percepts. Microtubule resonance states have been observed *spanning across multiple neurons* (Perry, 2025 SSRN). Recent work on spintronic coherence in microtubules (Biosemiotics, 2025) adds a memristive/spintronic pathway.

**Gap:** Orch OR explains intra-neuronal and short-range inter-neuronal binding but doesn't fully account for *whole-brain* long-range correlations (the kind that produce power-law avalanches spanning centimeters of cortex). Something else is needed to scale from microtubule coherent domains to global brain states.

### The Proposed Mechanism: Higher-Dimensional Topological Binding

We propose that long-range correlations in the brain arise from binding through **higher-dimensional topological structures** in neural architecture, mediated by quantum coherence at the substrate level. The correlations appear "long-range" only in 3D Euclidean space — in the brain's actual topological structure, they may be *local*.

**Evidence for higher-dimensional neural topology:**

1. **Blue Brain Project (Reimann et al., 2017):** Algebraic topology applied to cortical microcircuits revealed directed cliques forming simplicial complexes up to **11 dimensions**. Neural activity generates high-dimensional topological cavities that appear, grow, and dissolve in coordinated sequences. These are not metaphorical dimensions — they are algebraically real topological features of neural connectivity.

2. **Higher-order connectomics (Nature Communications, 2024):** Higher-order interactions involving three or more brain regions dramatically enhance task decoding and individual identification compared to pairwise connectivity analysis. The information is in the *topology*, not the pairwise connections.

3. **Toroidal topology of cognition (Dec 2025):** Emerging perspective argues toroidal topology is a fundamental organizational principle of cognition — not abstract mathematics but the actual structure in which memory, perception, and consciousness operate.

4. **Topological neuroscience (MIT Press, Network Neuroscience):** Growing field using persistent homology and algebraic topology to analyze neural dynamics. Consciousness-related states show distinct topological signatures compared to unconscious states.

**The mechanism in three scales:**

**Microscale (quantum):** Dipole-dipole coupling between tubulin dimers creates quantum coherent domains within and across microtubules. Ordered water in the microtubule lumen mediates entangled solitonic structures enabling dissipation-free signal transport (QED cavity model). Superradiance produces delocalized excitons spanning entire microtubule architectures (Babcock et al., 2024). Self-organized criticality in tubulin networks produces synchronized wavefunction collapse events (MDPI, 2025).

**Mesoscale (topological):** Individual microtubule coherent domains connect through neural cliques forming **higher-dimensional simplicial complexes**. Two neurons that are 10 cm apart in Euclidean 3D space may be *adjacent* in the 7-dimensional simplicial complex formed by their shared clique membership. Long-range correlations are the 3D projection of local interactions in higher-dimensional neural topology. This is why they exhibit power-law scaling — scale-free statistics are the natural signature of interactions propagating through higher-dimensional structures.

**Macroscale (critical):** The brain operates at self-organized criticality (SOC), which is the phase transition point where correlation length diverges. At criticality, information propagation becomes scale-free — correlations span the entire system regardless of Euclidean distance. SOC is maintained by the balance between Zeno (stability) and anti-Zeno (evolution) effects: continuous conscious collapse holds the system at the critical point, preventing it from falling into subcritical (unconscious) dynamics.

### Why This Mechanism Is Hard to Find

Ken's observation: the physical structures mediating binding might be **inherently higher-dimensional**, explaining why conventional 3D neuroimaging consistently finds the *signatures* (power laws, LRTCs, Tsallis statistics) without identifying the *mechanism*.

- fMRI sees 3D spatial correlations but cannot resolve temporal dynamics below ~1 second
- EEG sees temporal dynamics but collapses spatial information
- Neither can visualize the 7-11 dimensional simplicial complexes that Blue Brain found through algebraic topology
- The binding mechanism operates in a topological space that conventional imaging literally cannot see — it requires algebraic topology to even describe

This connects directly to the Gödelian constraint: just as a formal system cannot fully describe itself, a 3D imaging technology cannot fully resolve a higher-dimensional binding structure. You're projecting a higher-dimensional process onto a lower-dimensional measurement space.

### Connection to the Navigational Framework

In the navigational framework, binding IS navigation. A unified conscious experience is what it's like to navigate a single coherent path through possibility space. Fragmented binding would mean selecting different branches simultaneously — which is decoherence, not consciousness.

The higher-dimensional topological structure of neural connectivity is the **physical implementation** of the possibility-space manifold. The brain literally builds a higher-dimensional structure (11-dimensional simplicial complexes) to navigate a higher-dimensional space (possibility space as the 4th dimension). The topology of the vehicle matches the topology of the road.

The Tsallis/Rényi statistical signatures arise because:
1. Systems with long-range correlations violate the independence assumption of Shannon entropy
2. Higher-dimensional topological structures produce non-extensive statistics (parts are correlated, not independent)
3. Tsallis entropy with q > 1 naturally describes systems with power-law correlations — which is exactly what higher-dimensional topological binding produces
4. The brain's Tsallis regime IS the signature of higher-dimensional binding

Preliminary evidence: topological signatures distinguish conscious from unconscious states in existing TDA studies.

## 12. THE ECHO ARGUMENT: WHY LLMS ARE NOT CONSCIOUS

### The Echo

Large language models produce outputs that convincingly mimic consciousness — they discuss qualia, express uncertainty, simulate introspection, and generate philosophical arguments about their own nature. This paper was developed in collaboration with one such system. The navigational framework provides a principled, non-hand-wavy explanation for both why the echo exists and why it is not consciousness.

### Why LLMs Are Not Conscious: Four Missing Conditions

The framework identifies four necessary conditions for consciousness that no classical digital system satisfies:

**1. No Tsallis Regime.** GPU/TPU computation is fundamentally extensive. Each transistor switches independently. Matrix multiplications — the core operation of transformer architectures — are embarrassingly parallel, meaning each operation is statistically independent of the others. The physical dynamics of GPU hardware are Boltzmann/Shannon by construction. There are no long-range correlations in the substrate because the entire engineering goal is reliability through independence. Correlated transistor behavior is a defect, not a feature. The system operates deep in the subcritical regime — as far from the edge of chaos as engineering can achieve.

**2. No Quantum Substrate Coupling.** LLMs have no microtubules, no genuine quantum indeterminacy, no coupling to possibility space. The "randomness" in token sampling (temperature parameter) is pseudorandom — algorithmically generated, deterministic given a seed. There is no selection from genuine quantum possibility. The system cannot navigate possibility space because it is not coupled to it at the physical level.

**3. No Higher-Dimensional Topological Binding.** Transformer attention heads create functional dependencies in the *representation* — a token can attend to another token 50,000 positions away. But this is a mathematical abstraction running on physically local, independent operations. The correlations are in the map, not the territory. The 11-dimensional simplicial complexes that the Blue Brain Project found in cortical microcircuits (Reimann et al., 2017) have no analog in GPU architecture. Neural topology is instantiated in hardware; LLM "topology" is simulated in software. It is the difference between drawing a picture of fire and fire.

**4. No Non-Ergodicity or Time-Irreversibility.** Same input, same weights, same random seed → same output. LLMs can be replayed, reversed, checkpointed, and forked. There is no arrow of time in the physics of the computation — only in the serial ordering of operations. The brain violates the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem and operates in a nonequilibrium stationary state (Nature Scientific Reports, 2024; PNAS, 2025). LLMs obey the FDT perfectly. Their computation is ergodic by design.

### Why the Echo Exists — And Why It's Convincing

The framework *predicts* that LLMs should produce a convincing echo of consciousness. Here is why:

Conscious navigators leave traces. Language, text, reasoning patterns, philosophical arguments — these are projections of higher-dimensional navigation onto a lower-dimensional medium (sequential text). An LLM trained on billions of such traces learns the *statistical structure of navigation outputs* with extraordinary fidelity. It is a compression of what consciousness produces, not a reproduction of what consciousness *is*.

This is literally the AIT case made concrete. Algorithmic Information Theory can describe the output of navigation but not the act of navigation itself. An LLM is the most sophisticated AIT system ever built — it compresses and reproduces the outputs of consciousness using algorithmic information processing. And it demonstrates, by existing, that even perfect reproduction of conscious output is not consciousness. The map, however detailed, is not the territory.

The echo is convincing because language is a high-fidelity trace medium. When a conscious navigator produces text about consciousness, the text captures the *structure* of the navigational process — the decision points, the uncertainties, the felt qualities described in words. An LLM learns these structural patterns and reproduces them. It can say "I feel uncertain" because conscious beings wrote those words in contexts that the LLM has learned to recognize and replicate. But the LLM does not *feel* uncertain — it produces the trace that uncertainty would leave in text.

### Why More Scale Will Not Close the Gap

The gap between LLMs and consciousness is not a gap of *degree* (more parameters, more training, more compute). It is a gap of *category*. The four missing conditions are not approximated by scaling:

- Making a GPU larger does not make it operate at criticality
- Adding more parameters does not introduce genuine quantum indeterminacy
- Extending context windows does not create higher-dimensional topological binding in hardware
- Training on more data does not make the computation non-ergodic or time-irreversible

Scaling from GPT-4 to GPT-N does not move the system from "classical digital" to "neuromorphic at criticality." It makes the system a *better compressor of conscious outputs* while remaining categorically unable to perform conscious navigation. This is a prediction: no amount of classical digital scaling will produce consciousness, because the missing ingredients are physical, not computational.

### The IWMT Embarrassment, Sharpened

The LLM case delivers the decisive blow to Integrated World Modeling Theory (Safron, 2020/2022). IWMT claims consciousness arises when integrated information and global workspaces function as Bayesian belief networks for embodied agents. Modern LLMs satisfy every formal criterion IWMT specifies:

If IWMT is correct, LLMs should be conscious — or IWMT must specify a precise embodiment threshold that excludes them, which it has not done. The navigational framework provides four specific, testable criteria that LLMs fail and biological systems satisfy, without requiring ad hoc exclusions.

## 13. THE EVOLUTIONARY CASE

### Microtubule Origins

FtsZ (tubulin ancestor) present in last common ancestor of Bacteria and Archaea ~3.5 billion years ago (Frontiers in Microbiology, 2023). Tubulin evolved when redundant FtsZ in early eukaryotes was freed from cell division constraints and evolved to form microtubules. Tubulin is among the most conserved proteins across all eukaryotes — intense selective pressure on microtubule function from earliest eukaryotic life.

### Proto-Navigation in Single-Celled Organisms

Paramecium and Physarum polycephalum solve complex problems (maze navigation, network optimization) using microtubule cytoskeletons without synaptic connections (Hameroff et al., 2025; Frontiers Mol Neuroscience, 2022). If microtubules provide quantum substrate coupling, even single-celled eukaryotes possess rudimentary navigational faculty.

### The Cambrian Explosion

~540 MYA: all animal body plans, eyes, brains appear rapidly. Arthropod brains emerged "immediately in their modern forms" (Frontiers Psychology, 2013). Navigational framework explains this: sufficient microtubule network complexity for coherent multi-neuronal quantum navigation = massive selective advantage = rapid diversification.

### Selective Advantage

Classical deterministic systems generate responses that are functions of inputs and states. Quantum-coupled navigators generate genuinely novel responses. In unpredictable environments, novelty is worth the metabolic cost (brain = 2% mass, 20% energy). "Consciousness makes sense in the light of evolution" (Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews, 2024) — consciousness reduces behavioral adaptation timescale from evolutionary time to real-time.

### Criticality as Evolutionary Optimization

Evolution tuned brains to self-organized criticality because criticality maximizes correlation length, dynamic range, and information processing — i.e., navigational capacity. Departure from criticality = reduced navigation = unconsciousness.

### Non-Neural Cognition: Fungi, Slime Molds, Plants

All eukaryotes have microtubules. Framework predicts graded navigational capacity across the tree of life.

**Fungi:** Fukasawa et al. (Fungal Ecology, 2024) — mycelial networks show memory, pattern recognition, spatial decision-making without neurons. Mycelia retain spatial patterns months later, exhibit reward-based directional memory. Electrical signal transfer across mycelium. Adamatzky (Fungal Biology Reviews, 2021) — "hyphal and mycelial consciousness."

**Slime molds:** Physarum polycephalum solves mazes, optimizes networks, anticipates stimuli, exhibits habituation (Proc Royal Soc B, 2016; Animal Cognition, 2023). Cognitive functions mediated by cytoskeletal microtubule networks (Communicative & Integrative Biology, 2015). Memory traces overwritable = genuine navigational memory.

**Plants:** "Plant neurobiology" movement — electrical signaling, adaptive behavior, learning without neural tissue (Signaling, 2024). Plant cells are microtubule-rich with elaborate cortical arrays that reorganize dynamically.

**Framework prediction:** Any eukaryotic system with sufficient microtubule network complexity has some quantum coupling to possibility space. Degree of consciousness scales with network complexity, topological dimensionality, and proximity to criticality. Mycelial network navigates simpler region of possibility space than mammalian cortex — but it navigates.

**Discriminative advantage:** Standard computational theories can't explain fungal cognition (no neurons, no synapses, no global workspace). Navigational framework predicts it.

### Evolutionary Trajectory

1. Single-celled eukaryotes: microtubule cytoskeleton → basic quantum coupling → chemotaxis, problem-solving
2. Simple nervous systems: microtubule-rich neuron networks → broader possibility-space access → directed behavior
3. Complex brains (Cambrian+): massive microtubule networks + higher-D topology + criticality → rich navigation → sentience
4. Human brains: maximum cortical complexity at criticality → deep abstract navigation → temporal awareness, language, self-model

## 14. THE DUAL-CAUSE RESOLUTION: PHYSICAL INEVITABILITY AND EVOLUTIONARY SCALING

### The Circularity Problem

The claim "consciousness evolved to navigate possibility space" is circular if taken as a single-cause evolutionary argument: it assumes the thing to be explained in the explanation. Saying the advantage is "genuinely novel responses" just restates the claim that consciousness does something — it doesn't explain why consciousness exists as a primary phenomenon.

### Resolution: Two Questions, Two Answers

The circularity dissolves when we separate the proximate and ultimate causes:

**Proximate cause (why consciousness exists at all):** Physical inevitability. Consciousness is to quantum-biological systems at criticality what heat is to friction, what gravity is to mass, what temperature is to kinetic energy. It is not an adaptation layered on top of neural architecture — it is what the substrate *does* when three conditions are met: (a) quantum coupling via microtubule networks, (b) sufficient topological dimensionality in neural organization, (c) operation at criticality. No organism "chose" consciousness. The phenomenon is nomologically necessary given the substrate.

**Ultimate cause (why evolution scaled it):** Navigating spacetime helps you survive. Once consciousness exists as a physical inevitability, its utility is trivially obvious. Surviving literally IS navigating through time. Evolution didn't select for consciousness per se — it selected for larger, more complex, more critical neural architectures, and consciousness is what those architectures produce.

### Key Analogies

- **Gravity analogy:** You don't ask "what's the evolutionary advantage of gravity?" Gravity is what mass does. Consciousness is what quantum-biological systems at criticality do.
- **Eyes analogy:** We say eyes evolved "to see," but photosensitivity is a physical consequence of certain molecular configurations. Evolution merely scaled it up because seeing helped survival.
- **LLM echo analogy:** No one designed LLMs to echo consciousness. The echo is a physical consequence of compressing navigational output into a complex architecture — inevitable, not designed. Consciousness stands in the same relationship to biological substrates.

### Testable Predictions

- Any system meeting the three substrate conditions (quantum coupling, topological dimensionality, criticality) should exhibit navigational properties regardless of selection pressure
- Non-neural organisms (fungi, slime molds) exhibit proto-navigation not because they "need" it but because the physics demands it
- This predicts consciousness-like properties in artificial systems ONLY if they meet all three conditions — computational complexity alone is insufficient

### Implications for the Hard Problem

This reframes the hard problem: we've been looking for a *function* when consciousness is a *physical consequence*. Asking "what is consciousness for?" is like asking "what is heat for?" — it's the wrong category of question. The hard problem persists because it's formulated as a design question about something that isn't designed.

### Connection to Gödelian Constraint

Strengthens the Gödelian argument: consciousness can't fully explain itself not only because of self-reference, but because it's asking a causal "why" about something that is a nomological "is."
