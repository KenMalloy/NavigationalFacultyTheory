## Framing

A central ambiguity in your question is that “criticality” is not one thing: different communities operationalize it via (i) avalanche statistics and branching parameters, (ii) long‑range temporal correlations (LRTC) and 1/f scaling, (iii) proximity to bifurcations in whole‑brain models, or (iv) statistical physics mappings (e.g., Ising temperature). citeturn0search7turn16view0turn7view1turn18view1 These can yield apparently contradictory “toward/away from criticality” claims unless you keep the operational definition fixed.

For this review, the most discriminative operationalization for your **NFT prediction** is an *inverted‑U amplification bridge*: amplification from a hypothesized microscale driver to neural-scale dynamics is maximal near a critical point and falls off on both sides. This is not uniquely “quantum”: it is a generic feature of many criticality and bifurcation frameworks (maximal susceptibility near critical points). citeturn16view1turn6search5 What makes your NFT prediction discriminative is the additional claim that: during ego death, **classical entropy increases** while **quantum‑specific signatures decrease/vanish** because the amplification bridge fails away from criticality.

The main relevant competing framework is the entropic brain / REBUS family associated with entity["people","Robin Carhart-Harris","psychedelics researcher"] and entity["people","Karl Friston","free energy principle"]. REBUS explicitly states psychedelics increase entropy of spontaneous brain activity and relax high‑level priors, and it introduces the idea of an “upper and lower bound” (a “critical zone”) rather than unconstrained monotonicity. citeturn8view3turn5search4 Thus, “entropy increases but critical structure can degrade at extremes” is not automatically inconsistent with REBUS; the discriminative leverage requires measuring *which* aspects change with ego dissolution and whether any “quantum‑marker” family changes in the predicted direction. citeturn8view3turn18view1

Phenomenologically, ego dissolution is typically measured with instruments such as the Ego‑Dissolution Inventory (EDI) or relevant VAS items; the EDI validation paper is widely used as a measurement anchor. citeturn4search7

## Evidence for psychedelics shifting the brain toward or past criticality

### Broad “entropy/complexity increase” results (robust across drugs and modalities)
Multiple human studies show increased spontaneous signal diversity during psychedelics:
- MEG under psilocybin, LSD (and ketamine) shows reliably higher spontaneous “signal diversity,” with Lempel–Ziv complexity strongly contributing and correlations to subjective intensity. citeturn7view5
- DMT EEG work (2019) reports widespread alpha/beta decreases and significant increases in Lempel–Ziv signal diversity; temporal dynamics track intensity ratings. citeturn11view0
- Simultaneous EEG‑fMRI under DMT (2023 PNAS) reports widespread increases in Lempel–Ziv signal diversity under DMT, and positive correlations between intensity ratings and LZ global measures; it also links LZ to experiential richness. citeturn11view1
- Network-level fMRI analyses under LSD show increased global functional connectivity correlating with ego dissolution and reduced modularity/rich‑club structure, consistent with “flattened landscape / increased cross‑talk” narratives. citeturn8view4

These findings are consistent with “more diverse/less constrained” dynamics but, by themselves, do not establish *supercriticality* in a branching‑process sense—nor do they distinguish “near‑critical with maximal complexity” from “away from criticality but more random.” citeturn0search7turn18view1

### Direct “criticality” operationalizations: bifurcations, LRTC, and Ising mappings
A 2025 DMT whole‑brain model paper explicitly parametrizes a *time‑dependent bifurcation parameter* in Stuart–Landau oscillators, where proximity to the bifurcation (a=0) corresponds to “dynamic criticality,” and it reports that fitted dynamics move closer to the critical value around peak DMT effects. citeturn16view0turn16view1 This paper also emphasizes maximal sensitivity near the bifurcation and frames “critical slowing” as a signature of proximity to criticality. citeturn16view0turn16view1

By contrast, a 2026 human EEG study of DMT directly measures LRTC with a DFA exponent and finds DMT drives theta/alpha/beta oscillatory amplitude dynamics **away from criticality**, while using an additional functional E/I metric to infer the **direction** is subcritical. citeturn18view1turn18view0 It also highlights an important technical point: in its DFA-based framework, *entropy can increase away from criticality in both directions*, and “entropy” and “complexity” are inversely related operationally (white-noise-like → high entropy but low statistical complexity). citeturn8view1turn18view1

A different operationalization comes from an Ising‑model mapping of LSD fMRI (2023), reporting increased Ising temperature and algorithmic complexity under LSD and concluding that placebo brain dynamics were already above the Ising critical point (paramagnetic phase), with LSD shifting further away into a more disordered regime. citeturn7view1 This provides one of the clearer “over‑critical / more disordered” interpretations, but its meaning depends on whether the Ising mapping captures the same “criticality” relevant to avalanche/LRTC notions. citeturn0search7turn7view1

### Tsallis-q and power-law exponents in psychedelic states
No peer‑reviewed psychedelic studies were found that report a Tsallis **q‑parameter** as a primary criticality marker (in the specific “Tsallis q” sense you mention). Tsallis entropy has been used in EEG in other contexts (e.g., burst suppression), showing the technique exists, but this does not substitute for psychedelic-state q estimates. citeturn3search0turn3search4

## Ego dissolution as “departure from criticality” versus “enhanced criticality”

### Strongest direct evidence: DMT self‑dissolution correlates with a subcritical shift
The 2026 DMT criticality paper is unusually aligned with your discriminative interest because it does **all** of the following:
- Measures distance from criticality using DFA exponents in theta/alpha/beta oscillations. citeturn18view3
- Finds DMT decreases DFA exponents (shift away from criticality) across scalp regions. citeturn18view3
- Uses a functional E/I ratio metric to infer the shift is **toward subcritical regimes**, not supercritical. citeturn18view0turn18view1
- Shows that criticality shifts in theta/alpha correlate with a self‑dissolution VAS item (“disintegration of my usual sense of self or ego”), with reported correlations in theta and alpha bands (e.g., r ≈ −0.61 and −0.56 in their summary). citeturn18view1turn18view2
- Interprets the shift as “more entropic, less complex” in its operational definition. citeturn18view1turn8view1

This is direct empirical support for the idea that intense self disruption can coincide with **loss of near‑critical temporal structure**, even while some “entropy-like” measures increase. citeturn18view1turn8view1

### Extreme ego dissolution case: 5‑MeO‑DMT and low-dimensional stabilization
A 2025 high‑dose 5‑MeO‑DMT EEG study (29 participants, 12 mg vaporized) reports that low‑frequency traveling waves become disorganized and cease canonical forward/backward travel; importantly, it finds broadband dynamics “more stable and low-dimensional” on a “steepened energy landscape,” and it explicitly frames this as a distinctive effect relative to other tryptamine psychedelics. citeturn15view0 This “low-dimensional steady state” signature is—if anything—more consistent with a departure from “maximally metastable/critical” regimes than with a simple “more complexity everywhere” story. citeturn15view0

### LSD/psilocybin ego dissolution correlates: network disintegration/integration, not criticality per se
For LSD, increased global functional connectivity correlates with ego dissolution and reduced modularity of networks. citeturn8view4  
For psilocybin, ego dissolution correlates with reduced medial temporal lobe–cortex connectivity, salience network disintegration, and reduced interhemispheric communication. citeturn14view2  
These are robust “ego dissolution ↔ network reconfiguration” results, but they do not directly tell you whether the system is subcritical, critical, or supercritical in a physically grounded sense. citeturn0search7turn14view2turn8view4

### Dose-response and “ego death doses”
Large controlled dose-response datasets for subjective psilocybin effects exist (including dose-dependent ego dissolution), but most do not include criticality markers measured concurrently, leaving a key missing link for your question about low-dose “more critical” vs high-dose “supercritical/subcritical” regimes. citeturn0search2turn0search21

## PCI, Lempel–Ziv, and other complexity measures during ego dissolution

### Lempel–Ziv (signal diversity): widely measured; ego dissolution correlations exist but depend on context
The 2017 MEG study reports increased signal diversity under LSD/psilocybin and correlations with intensity. citeturn7view5  
The 2019 and 2023 DMT EEG studies show increased Lempel–Ziv complexity and correlations with real-time intensity and/or experiential richness. citeturn11view0turn11view1

A particularly relevant 2024 LSD study manipulates “setting” (eyes closed/open, music, video) and shows that LSD increases LZ-based “brain entropy” across conditions, but the effect is largest eyes-closed; it also reports significant positive correlations between ego dissolution and entropy in DMN regions under eyes-closed conditions. citeturn9view0turn9view2turn9view3 Importantly, it notes that participants watching a video had the highest absolute brain entropy yet did not give maximal subjective ratings—suggesting scalar entropy measures do not map monotonically onto “ego death” or “intensity.” citeturn9view0turn9view3

### PCI (perturbational complexity): psilocybin may not increase it
A 2023 psilocybin study combining TMS‑EEG with resting EEG reports that **perturbational complexity is unaltered**, while spontaneous EEG (LZ complexity) changes. citeturn14view0 This implies that psychedelic phenomenology can change dramatically without a corresponding increase in the kind of “causal/perturbational” complexity PCI is meant to capture, at least under that protocol. citeturn14view0

This matters for your discriminative prediction because PCI-like measures may track *global integrative causal capacity* differently than spontaneous entropy measures; a decoupling between “signal diversity up” and “perturbational complexity unchanged or down” is a plausible “bridge failure” proxy—even before any quantum-specific marker is measured. citeturn14view0turn18view1

### A key technical reconciliation: “entropy up” can coincide with “complexity down”
The 2026 DMT criticality paper explicitly warns that entropy/complexity/criticality relationships are nontrivial and that, in its DFA-based framing, higher entropy can correspond to more white-noise-like activity farther from criticality and lower statistical complexity. citeturn8view1turn18view1 This is a central interpretive hinge: it allows observations like “signal diversity goes up” to coexist with “criticality structure goes down,” depending on which measure you’re using. citeturn18view1turn7view5

## DMN breakdown during ego death and its relationship to criticality metrics

The DMN/self story is empirically strong: LSD increases global integration and reduces modular organization, correlating with ego dissolution. citeturn8view4 Psilocybin ego dissolution correlates with disruptions linking medial temporal systems, salience network integrity, and interhemispheric communication (often discussed in relation to DMN/MTL coupling hypotheses). citeturn14view2

However, **DMN breakdown ↔ explicit criticality departure** is still under-measured. The 2024 LSD “external stimulation” paper provides a partial bridge by relating ego dissolution to posterior DMN entropy (LZ) and shows that context can collapse the brain–phenomenology correlation even when entropy is high. citeturn9view0turn9view2turn9view3  
Meanwhile, the strongest ego-dissolution–criticality link in the literature is currently frequency-band based (theta/alpha DFA + fE/I) under DMT, not explicitly DMN-functional-connectivity based. citeturn18view1turn18view0

A fair assessment is: “DMN disintegration correlates with ego dissolution” is strongly supported; “DMN disintegration marks supercriticality/subcriticality” is not yet strongly supported by direct, concurrent measurement, and is an open opportunity for a discriminative test. citeturn14view2turn8view4turn18view1

## Quantum probability signatures during altered states and discriminative tests for NFT

### What exists (but not in psychedelics)
Quantum cognition provides operational “quantum probability” signatures in decision-making, including order effects and the QQ equality (and related constraints), validated on large behavioral datasets in unimpaired populations. citeturn2search3

### What is missing
No peer‑reviewed studies were found that administer QQ‑equality / order-effect paradigms—or conjunction-fallacy–style quantum probability tasks—*during psychedelic states* (or explicitly during ego dissolution episodes), despite extensive psychedelic psychometrics. (This is a literature absence statement; the closest “order effects” in psychedelic trials concern treatment order in cross-over designs, which do not test quantum-cognition signatures.) citeturn4search1turn2search3

### How to make NFT’s “quantum markers disappear at ego death” prediction testable
A scientifically tractable version of your discriminative prediction (NFT vs entropic-brain-style “uniform entropy up”) would be:

- **Within-subject, dose-titrated design** (e.g., placebo → moderate dose → high dose approaching ego dissolution), with continuous ego dissolution ratings (EDI and/or VAS) and concurrent neurophysiology. citeturn4search7turn18view2  
- **Neural criticality panel**: DFA/LRTC in alpha/theta (as in the DMT criticality study), plus avalanche-based measures where feasible (EEG/MEG avalanche distributions, branching parameter m), plus perturbational complexity (TMS‑EEG PCI). citeturn18view1turn14view0turn6search12  
- **Quantum-cognition task battery**: standardized QQ equality/order effect tasks and/or other noncommutative-choice signatures, administered during plateau and/or with brief task blocks synchronized to peak intensity. citeturn2search3  

**NFT-consistent pattern (operational):** classical signal diversity (LZ) increases with dose/intensity, while *near-critical amplification markers* (DFA exponent toward ~1, avalanche criticality, PCI) show an inverted-U or a drop at high ego dissolution; quantum-cognition signatures (if present at baseline) weaken or become more classically consistent specifically at ego dissolution extremes. citeturn18view1turn9view0turn14view0  
**Entropic-brain-consistent pattern (simplified):** broad entropy/diversity measures increase with dose/intensity and remain the dominant correlate; criticality markers trend toward a “critical zone” but do not specifically collapse at ego dissolution. citeturn8view3turn9view3  

Because REBUS already admits an upper/lower bound (critical zone), the strongest discriminative edge would come not from “entropy vs criticality” alone, but from whether a distinct, reproducible set of *quantum‑cognition behavioral signatures* systematically changes with ego dissolution in a way not explained by generic attention/executive disruption. citeturn8view3turn2search3
