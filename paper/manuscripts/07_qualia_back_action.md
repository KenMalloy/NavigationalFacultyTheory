## Scope and Non-Claims

This paper does not solve the hard problem. It does not stand as an empirical paper on its own. It does not derive consciousness from quantum mechanics, and it does not prove that brains exploit laboratory-style measurement processes. What follows is a conjectural constraints proposal, not an evidenced biological mechanism. Its value, if any, is as a sharp philosophical constraints essay that narrows the search space and makes falsifiable commitments -- not as empirical science.

## Abstract

This paper should not present itself as a solved theory of qualia. Its strongest publishable form is a constraints paper that asks whether subjective character can be given a more specific physical address than generic “computation” or generic “information processing.” The proposal is that measurement-like update processes with intrinsic disturbance, when integrated and globally propagated inside a self-modeling system, offer a conjectural candidate physical locus for qualitative character. The gain is not that the explanatory gap disappears. The gain is that the proposal makes commitments: the proposal commits to disturbance having a non-epiphenomenal role, implementation details matter, and there should be principled differences between systems that merely compute and systems that compute through structured back-action.

## Why A Physical Address Matters

The paper should say this plainly. Most consciousness theories describe correlates, architectures, or functions. Much fewer even attempt to specify where the phenomenal part of the process physically lives. The value of the back-action proposal is not that it explains away qualia. The value is that it narrows the search space from "whatever computation is going on" to a specific class of update processes in which information gain and disturbance are inseparable (for the relevant physics of measurement back-action, see [Hatridge et al. 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23307736/); for room-temperature observation of quantum back-action, see [Cripe et al. 2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1051-4)).

That is a relocation claim, not a dissolution claim. The manuscript gets stronger when it foregrounds that distinction instead of sounding like it has solved the hard problem. This paper's contribution is as a philosophical constraints essay. It proposes a conjectural physical address and specifies what would count against it. It does not provide empirical evidence that the proposed address is correct.

## Minimal Commitments

| Commitment | Why it matters | What would weaken it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Qualia must be tied to a physically instantiated update process | avoids leaving experience at the level of abstract function only | no coherent difference between pure computation and disturbance-bearing implementations |
| The relevant update process must include intrinsic disturbance | makes experience causally involved rather than epiphenomenal | disturbance can be removed without any change in reportable quality |
| Disturbance must be integrated and propagated | avoids reducing qualia to isolated microevents | disturbance signatures remain local and behaviorally inert |
| The proposal must yield discriminative consequences | prevents simple relabeling | no experiment or surrogate system can distinguish the view from generic functionalism |

These commitments are ontological commitments rather than supported findings. They define what the proposal bets on, not what has been demonstrated.

## Minimal Criterion For A Measurement-Like Update

The paper needs one concrete definition, even if provisional. What follows is a stipulative working definition, not a consensus standard or empirically derived criterion. A biologically relevant update should count as measurement-like only if it has all three features.

1. Information gain about a system state.
2. State disturbance that is not freely removable (see [Hatridge et al. 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23307736/) for direct experimental characterization of quantum measurement back-action; [Cripe et al. 2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1051-4) for observation of quantum back-action at room temperature).
3. Downstream control consequences that depend on the disturbance statistics, not only the decoded value.

Without this section, the phrase “measurement-like” stays too metaphorical.

## Why This Is Not Just Bayesian Updating

The paper needs one short contrast case. What follows is a conceptual contrast, not an empirical dissociation claim. It has not been experimentally tested.

- In ordinary Bayesian updating, a system may revise beliefs without any principled requirement that the represented world be disturbed by the update.
- In a measurement-like process, the update is physically coupled to disturbance.
- The back-action proposal claims that this disturbance-bearing implementation difference matters, not just the abstract input-output map.

That contrast is what keeps the paper from collapsing into a dressed-up functionalism.

## Discriminative Consequences

The conceptual paper becomes stronger if it names a few concrete consequences. These are predictions of the proposal, not established findings.

- The proposal commits to disturbance having a non-epiphenomenal role; it cannot be fully inert.
- The proposal predicts a possible phenomenal difference despite matched inputs and outputs, when the systems differ in disturbance statistics. This prediction should be stated hypothetically and should avoid presuming phenomenal facts about artificial systems.
- A true back-action-evasion analogue (see [Moller et al. 2017](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22980) for experimental back-action evasion in physics) would be a long-range thought-experimental discriminator, not an imminent experiment. It would separate information extraction from disturbance and thereby provide a principled test of the proposal's core commitment.

## Case Studies And Thought Experiments

### 1. Back-action-evasion limit case

Borrowing from the broader project, the cleanest limiting case is a system that preserves information extraction while progressively suppressing disturbance (see [Moller et al. 2017](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22980) for a physics demonstration of back-action evasion). If qualitative character tracks disturbance-bearing update rather than abstract computation alone, the proposal predicts that phenomenology should weaken as the process approaches back-action evasion. This is a long-range thought-experimental discriminator, not an imminent experiment; no current experimental platform can implement the required comparison in a biologically relevant system.

### 2. Matched-input, different-disturbance surrogate

Imagine two matched control systems exposed to the same input stream and producing similar output quality. One implements its internal update through a disturbance-bearing physical coupling; the other implements a disturbance-minimized emulation. If implementation details are truly irrelevant, the two systems should be phenomenally equivalent. The back-action proposal predicts a possible phenomenal difference despite matched inputs and outputs. This thought experiment should be stated hypothetically. We do not currently have the means to verify phenomenal facts about artificial systems, and the proposal should avoid presuming them.

### 3. Attention as disturbance shaping

The book materials already lean on the idea that selective attention changes not only what is processed but how strongly the underlying update perturbs the substrate. This is a possible case study, not a mechanistic claim. It links the abstract proposal to a familiar phenomenological contrast -- vivid, effortful awareness versus dim or peripheral awareness -- but does not constitute evidence that attention operates through disturbance shaping in any specific physical sense. Establishing that link would require independent empirical work beyond what this constraints essay provides.

## Failure Modes

The paper improves when it states what would count against it.

- no principled difference can be drawn between disturbance-bearing and disturbance-minimized implementations
- all apparent disturbance effects reduce to ordinary energy, sensory, or behavioral confounds
- the proposal never yields a discriminative prediction beyond generic embodiment or generic recurrent processing
