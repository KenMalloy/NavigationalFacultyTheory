# Consciousness as Navigational Faculty: A Unification of IIT, GNWT, and Orch OR Through Possibility Space

## Kenneth Malloy (Draft Outline v0.1 — March 2026)

---

## THESIS

Consciousness is not an emergent property of computation, an information-theoretic quantity, or a quantum collapse event. It is a **faculty** — analogous to vision or proprioception — that evolved to navigate the fourth dimension understood as the total possibility space of all 3D realities across all of time. This framing resolves longstanding conflicts between Integrated Information Theory, Global Neuronal Workspace Theory, and Orchestrated Objective Reduction by recasting them as descriptions of subsystems within a single navigational architecture, and addresses the quantum Zeno problem by reinterpreting continuous collapse as the mechanism of coherent temporal experience rather than an obstacle to it. The persistent incompleteness of internal theories of consciousness is predicted by this framework as a Gödelian consequence of any sufficiently complex system attempting self-description.

---

## I. THE INCOMPLETENESS OF EXISTING THEORIES

Why: Establishes the problem space. No current theory accounts for consciousness fully, and the most rigorous empirical test to date confirmed this.

### I.A. The Hard Problem Persists
Why: Frames the explanatory gap that motivates the paper.
- Chalmers' formulation of the hard problem remains unresolved after three decades (Chalmers, 1995)
- The explanatory gap between neural correlates and phenomenal experience has not narrowed despite advances in neuroimaging (Levine, 1983; Block, 2007)

### I.B. IIT and GNWT: Partial, Not Complete
Why: Shows the two leading theories each capture something real but fail empirically when tested head-to-head.
- IIT describes integration requirements but mistakes structural properties for consciousness itself (Tononi, 2004; Tononi et al., 2016)
- GNWT describes broadcasting but addresses access consciousness, not phenomenal consciousness (Baars, 1988; Dehaene & Changeux, 2011)
- COGITATE adversarial collaboration: neither theory's predictions fully validated (Cogitate Consortium, Nature 2025)
- Epiontic Consciousness Theory shows IIT and GNWT are neurobiologically connected, not competing (ECT gridography study, Nature Sci Reports 2025)

### I.C. The Gödelian Constraint
Why: Provides a principled reason to expect internal theories to fail, reframing the hard problem as a structural feature rather than a temporary gap.
- Gödel's incompleteness: sufficiently complex formal systems cannot fully describe themselves from within (Gödel, 1931)
- Every existing consciousness theory is a conscious mind attempting to reverse-engineer consciousness by examining its own outputs
- This predicts persistent incompleteness in any self-referential theory of consciousness (Hofstadter, 1979; Hoel, 2024)
- Implication: a complete theory requires describing consciousness by its *function* from a dimensional perspective, not its mechanism from within

## II. POSSIBILITY SPACE AND THE FOURTH DIMENSION

Why: Defines the ontological framework — what consciousness navigates.

### II.A. Time as Possibility Space
Why: Reinterprets the fourth dimension beyond the standard temporal line.
- Block universe / eternalism: all events (past, present, future) equally real in 4D spacetime (Minkowski, 1908; Putnam, 1967)
- The "hard problem of time": why does static spacetime generate subjective temporal flow? (Callender, 2017)
- Possibility space as the superset of all possible 3D configurations across all of time — not many-worlds, but the full landscape of what could be
- Compatible with QBism: quantum probabilities as subjective, describing the agent's relationship to possibility (Fuchs, 2010; Edwards, 2025)

### II.B. Entropy and the Arrow
Why: Grounds temporal experience in thermodynamics without requiring a naive arrow-of-time assumption.
- Entropy-driven awareness: consciousness as a consequence of structured entropy flow (Jha, PhilArchive)
- The entropic brain hypothesis: entropy levels track conscious states quantitatively (Carhart-Harris et al., 2014; 2023)
- Psychological arrow of time as thermodynamic asymmetry, not fundamental physics (Hemmo & Shenker, PhilArchive)
- Navigation requires a gradient — entropy provides the terrain of possibility space

## III. CONSCIOUSNESS AS NAVIGATIONAL FACULTY

Why: The core claim. Defines what consciousness *does* rather than what it *is*.

### III.A. Faculty, Not Epiphenomenon
Why: Establishes consciousness as functional — it does something that nothing else can do.
- Eyes are for photons, ears for pressure waves — consciousness is for navigating possibility space
- Consciousness selects reality from the space of possible realities, making it functional rather than decorative
- Distinct from Hoffman's interface theory (consciousness as desktop metaphor) by being active/selective rather than passive/representational (Hoffman, 2019)
- Fitness Beats Truth theorem supports the interface argument from evolutionary game theory — perception is compressed, not veridical (Hoffman et al., 2015)

### III.B. Navigation, Not Observation
Why: Critical distinction that resolves the Zeno problem and differentiates from passive consciousness-causes-collapse.
- Standard observer-collapse theories treat consciousness as passive measurement (Stapp, 1993; von Neumann, 1932)
- Navigation is active selection — steering through branching points in possibility space
- This distinction is not merely semantic: it generates different predictions about the relationship between consciousness and quantum evolution (see Section V)

### III.C. Substrate Coupling as Necessary Condition
Why: Explains why computational complexity alone is insufficient — you need quantum coupling.
- Classical computation cannot perform genuine path selection from quantum possibility space
- Biological consciousness requires coupling to quantum indeterminacy at the substrate level (Penrose, 1989; Penrose & Hameroff, 1996)
- This predicts that AI systems, regardless of complexity, lack consciousness absent quantum substrate coupling
- Recent evidence: superradiance in microtubule networks at biological temperatures (Babcock et al., 2024), anesthetic binding to microtubules disrupts consciousness (Khan et al., 2024)

## IV. THE VEHICLE: UNIFICATION OF IIT, GNWT, AND ORCH OR

Why: Shows the three major theories as subsystems of a single navigational architecture rather than competitors.

### IV.A. IIT as Chassis — The Structural Requirement
Why: Integration is necessary because navigation requires a unified decision-maker.
- Φ (integrated information) measures the structural capacity for unified selection (Tononi, 2004; 2016)
- A fragmented system would "choose" different branches simultaneously — decoherence, not navigation
- IIT's insight is real: integration is required. But Tononi mistakes the chassis for the driver.
- Formal derivation: IIT's six axioms derivable from a single categorical universal mapping property (Phillips & Tsuchiya, 2024)

### IV.B. GNWT as Dashboard — The Broadcasting Mechanism
Why: Once a navigational selection is made, it must propagate to the whole organism.
- Global broadcast makes the selected reality available to all cognitive subsystems for coherent action (Baars, 1988; Dehaene et al., 2011)
- Evolutionary origins: GNW evolved to coordinate diverse processing modules in early vertebrates (GNW evolutionary analysis, 2023)
- Broadcasting is essential for functional consciousness but describes process, not substance
- Neural synchronization: only perceived stimuli induce long-distance synchronization across brain regions (Melloni et al., 2007)

### IV.C. Orch OR as Steering-Road Coupling — The Quantum Interface
Why: The mechanism by which the navigator connects to possibility space.
- Orch OR provides the physical interface between consciousness and quantum indeterminacy (Penrose & Hameroff, 1996; 2014)
- Microtubules as quantum substrate: experimentally supported by anesthetic studies, superradiance, and energy migration evidence (Hameroff et al., 2025; Babcock et al., 2024; Oblinski et al., 2023)
- Conscious active inference via Orch OR accounts for discrete perceptual cycles (Safron et al., 2025)
- Orchestration = navigation. Consciousness doesn't randomly sample quantum states; it actively orchestrates collapse through biological structures.

### IV.D. Why They Coexist
Why: The synthesis — they were never competing.
- IIT, GNWT, and Orch OR describe different subsystems of the same vehicle
- Each is necessary but none is sufficient
- IWMT (Safron, 2020/2022) attempted this unification through the Free Energy Principle but fails the AI embodiment test and treats time as furniture (see Section VI)
- The navigational framework unifies through function (what consciousness does) rather than mechanism (how the brain computes)

## V. THE QUANTUM ZENO RESOLUTION

Why: Addresses the strongest formal objection to consciousness-causes-collapse.

### V.A. The Zeno Problem
Why: States the objection clearly.
- Quantum Zeno effect: continuous measurement prevents quantum evolution (Misra & Sudarshan, 1977)
- Chalmers & McQueen (2021): naive consciousness-collapse falsified by Zeno; additional Φ-dependent constraints required
- McQueen's fix adds rules — not parsimonious

### V.B. Continuous Collapse as Coherent Navigation
Why: Shows the navigational framework resolves Zeno without additional constraints.
- Continuous collapse does not freeze reality — it *produces* stable coherent experienced reality
- The Zeno effect is the mechanism of persistence: objects appear solid because consciousness continuously selects a coherent path
- Change arises because each collapse is a new selection — the continuity of collapse is the continuity of experience
- Film projector analogy: discrete frames at 40-80 Hz produce subjectively continuous navigation (Hameroff et al., 2025)

### V.C. The Anti-Zeno Effect
Why: Provides physics-level support for continuous collapse driving evolution rather than freezing it.
- Anti-Zeno effect: under certain coupling regimes, frequent measurement accelerates transitions (Kaulakys & Gontis, 1997)
- Zeno vs. anti-Zeno determined by relationship between measurement rate and environmental spectral density
- If neural quantum coupling has the right spectral profile, continuous conscious collapse drives reality forward
- Testable: spectral properties of microtubule quantum states should predict Zeno/anti-Zeno regime

## VI. ENGAGEMENT WITH COMPETING FRAMEWORKS

Why: Positions the theory against the strongest alternatives honestly.

### VI.A. IWMT — Implementation Without Nature
Why: Addresses the most sophisticated existing unification.
- IWMT combines IIT + GNWT + FEP + Active Inference under variational free energy minimization (Safron, 2020; 2022)
- Strength: single mathematical formalism, testable predictions, no quantum dependency
- Failure 1: AI systems (LLMs) satisfy IWMT's formal criteria — Bayesian inference, world modeling, prediction error minimization, physical substrate — yet are not conscious. Embodiment threshold unspecified.
- Failure 2: treats time as background parameter. Cannot account for subjective temporal flow or the existence of "now."
- Resolution: IWMT describes implementation; navigational framework describes nature. Potentially complementary.

### VI.B. Free Energy Principle — Minimization vs. Navigation
Why: Engages with Friston's framework, which is the closest mechanistic relative.
- FEP: organisms minimize surprise / free energy (Friston, 2010)
- Active inference: organisms act to fulfill predictions (Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, 2022)
- Friston (2018): "Consciousness is nothing more than inference about my future"
- Key distinction: FEP frames consciousness as *minimizing* surprise (defensive, homeostatic); navigational framework frames it as *selecting* from possibility (active, creative). May be the same process described inside-out (Friston) vs. outside-in (this paper).

### VI.C. Dimensional Approaches — Structure vs. Function
Why: Differentiates from the closest existing dimensional theories.
- Carr: 5D psychophysical space with mental time as extra dimension (Carr, 2015; 2021)
- Sjöstedt-Hughes: Hyperdimensional Neutral Monism — consciousness as "protrusion" into higher dimensions (Sjöstedt-Hughes, 2022)
- Both treat consciousness as structural. This framework treats it as functional. Protrusion is passive; navigation is active.

---

### VI.D. Strong Physicalism — The Kolmogorov Complexity Argument and Its Temporal Category Error
Why: Engages with the most rigorous pure physicalist position — and one with personal intellectual lineage.
- DeLancey (2023): consciousness is mysterious only because phenomenal experiences are Kolmogorov-incompressible; the explanatory gap is epistemic, not ontological
- McQueen (2023, NDPR review): DeLancey never estimates Kolmogorov Complexity of any experience; the incompressibility cost principle is unproven; K-complexity may be the wrong measure entirely
- The temporal absence: DeLancey's text contains zero substantive treatment of temporal experience, subjective time, or the arrow of time
- The double jump: Kolmogorov Complexity can (arguably) explain why qualia resist description (Jump 1), but cannot explain why experience has intrinsic temporal direction (Jump 2) because K-complexity is symmetric — K(x) ≈ K(reverse(x))
- The buck-shift pattern: dissolving mystery at one level (qualia → complexity) while facing an unreduced mystery at the next (time → ???). Same pattern as Dennett's "explaining away" (Chalmers, 1995), functionalist access/phenomenal gap (Block, 1995), identity theory correlation gap (Levine, 1983)
- Each iteration adds incredibility because the remaining mystery is harder, not easier
- Core argument: **any framework treating consciousness as a static property (complexity, information, integration) will fail to account for temporal experience. The navigational framework succeeds precisely here.**

### VI.E. The AIT Evolution and Its Limits — From Kolmogorov to Tsallis, and Beyond Computation
Why: Uses AIT's own internal development to argue DeLancey chose the wrong tool, then shows even the right tool is insufficient. Three-move argument structure.

**Move 1 (gentle): AIT outgrew Kolmogorov Complexity**
- Bennett (1988): added computation *time* (logical depth) because description length was insufficient for physical complexity
- Gell-Mann & Lloyd (1996): added *meaning* (effective complexity) — separating regularity from noise; observer-relative
- Zenil (2017-2020): added *dynamics and causality* (Algorithmic Information Dynamics) — applied to consciousness in Neuroscience of Consciousness (Oxford)
- Tsallis-Chaitin (arXiv:2602.03919, Feb 2026): non-local grammar constraints on Turing machines yield Tsallis entropy with power-law scaling instead of linear
- DeLancey used Stage 1 of a 5-stage evolution. AIT's own development is an implicit argument against his tool choice.

**Move 2 (sharp): Even advanced AIT is insufficient**
- AIT at every stage is a theory of Turing computation: deterministic, classical, discrete, substrate-independent
- Consciousness (per this framework) requires: genuine indeterminacy, substrate dependence (quantum coherence in microtubules), continuous temporal embedding, non-computability (Penrose, 1989)
- AIT can describe the *output* of navigation but not the *act* of navigation — because navigation requires genuine quantum selection, which Turing machines lack
- The Tsallis-Chaitin paper illustrates this: non-local grammar constraints are *imposed on* the machine from outside. Consciousness *is* the thing imposing constraints on which possibilities are realized.

**Move 3 (empirical): The neuroscience confirms the brain exceeds AIT's regime**
- Brain operates at self-organized criticality; unconsciousness = departure from criticality (Nature Comms Biology, 2024)
- Brain is non-ergodic and time-irreversible — nonequilibrium stationary state (Nature Sci Reports, 2024; PNAS, 2025)
- Tsallis entropy describes systems with long-range interactions, multifractal constraints, long-term memory — all characteristic of neural dynamics
- Rényi entropy outperforms Shannon for tracking consciousness states in EEG (Frontiers Comp Neuro, 2024)
- The Kolmogorov-Shannon equivalence *formally breaks* for Tsallis and Rényi entropies (Teixeira et al., 2011)
- DeLancey brought a Shannon ruler to a Tsallis world

## VII. THE BINDING MECHANISM: HIGHER-DIMENSIONAL TOPOLOGICAL BINDING

Why: Proposes a specific physical mechanism for how consciousness produces unified experience from distributed neural activity.

### VII.A. The Binding Problem Restated
Why: Long-range correlations (LRTCs, power-law avalanches, Tsallis statistics) are the *signatures* of binding, not the mechanism.
- Classical gamma synchrony describes what binding looks like, not how it works (Melloni et al., 2007)
- Orch OR solves intra-neuronal binding via quantum coherence but doesn't fully account for whole-brain binding (Hameroff et al., 2025)
- The mechanism must scale from microtubule coherent domains (nanometers) to cortical integration (centimeters)

### VIII.B. Higher-Dimensional Neural Topology
Why: The brain literally builds higher-dimensional structures.
- Blue Brain Project: neural cliques form simplicial complexes up to 11 algebraic dimensions (Reimann et al., 2017)
- Higher-order connectomics: 3+ region interactions dramatically outperform pairwise analysis for task decoding (Nature Communications, 2024)
- Toroidal topology as fundamental organizational principle of cognition (2025)
- Correlations that appear "long-range" in 3D Euclidean space may be *local* in the brain's actual higher-dimensional topology

### VIII.C. The Three-Scale Mechanism
Why: Connects quantum substrate to global binding through topological intermediary.
- **Microscale (quantum):** Dipole-dipole coupling, superradiance (Babcock et al., 2024), QED cavity effects in microtubule lumens → coherent domains of 1-10 ms
- **Mesoscale (topological):** Coherent domains connect through neural cliques forming higher-dimensional simplicial complexes → long-range correlations as projections of local higher-dimensional interactions
- **Macroscale (critical):** Self-organized criticality maintains divergent correlation length; continuous conscious collapse holds the system at the critical point (Zeno = stability, anti-Zeno = evolution)

### VIII.D. Why the Mechanism Is Hidden
Why: Explains why decades of neuroimaging haven't found it.
- 3D imaging cannot resolve higher-dimensional topological structures (requires algebraic topology)
- Connects to the Gödelian constraint: projecting a higher-dimensional process onto lower-dimensional measurement space necessarily loses structure
- The topology of the vehicle (11-D neural simplicial complexes) matches the topology of the road (possibility space)

### VII.E. Predictions from the Binding Mechanism
Why: Makes the mechanism testable.
- Persistent homology (Betti numbers) should track consciousness level
- Anesthetics should reduce topological dimensionality before reducing activity amplitude
- Microtubule-disrupting drugs should collapse topology before collapsing activity
- Effective dimension of neural topology should be maximal at criticality

## VIII. TOWARD FORMALIZATION

Why: Addresses the formalism gap. A theory without math is a metaphor.

### VIII.A. Riemannian Geometry — Geodesic Path Selection (Mesoscale)
Why: The most intuitive formalization for navigation.
- Possibility space as Riemannian manifold; consciousness navigates along geodesics (Lu, 2024)
- Steering = adjusting tangent vectors; collapse = path determination at branch points
- Testable: conscious neural trajectories should be geodesic; unconscious trajectories non-geodesic
- Connects to existing neural state-space models and brain dynamics research

### VIII.B. Hilbert Space Projections (Microscale)
Why: The most physically grounded formalization.
- Cognitive states as vectors in Hilbert space; decisions as projection operators (Busemeyer & Bruza, 2012)
- Navigation = projection onto subspaces; Born rule gives selection probabilities
- Empirically supported: human cognition violates classical probability, follows quantum probability (Busemeyer & Bruza, 2012; 2023-2024)
- If Orch OR correct, navigation literally IS projection in Hilbert space

### VIII.C. Category Theory — Universal Properties (Macroscale)
Why: Provides the structural framework relating the subsystems.
- Adjoint functors formalize the subjective-objective gap (Prentner, 2024)
- IIT axioms derivable from single universal mapping property (Phillips & Tsuchiya, 2024)
- Three subsystems (IIT, GNWT, Orch OR) as categories; consciousness as the universal property making the diagram commute
- Gödel constraint naturally expressed: self-referential categories have inherent incompleteness

### VIII.D. Multi-Scale Synthesis
Why: The complete picture may require all three.
- Hilbert space (microscale quantum) → Riemannian manifold (mesoscale neural) → Category theory (macroscale structural)
- Related by structure-preserving functors across scales
- Consciousness State Space (Berkovich-Ohana & Glicksohn, 2014) as existing model of consciousness-as-trajectory

## IX. THE ECHO: WHY LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS ARE NOT CONSCIOUS

Why: Uses the most sophisticated AI systems as a case study, demonstrates the framework's discriminative power, and delivers the decisive blow to competing theories.

### IX.A. The Echo of Consciousness
Why: Explains why LLM outputs mimic consciousness without being conscious.
- LLMs are trained on projections of conscious navigation onto text — they learn the statistical structure of navigation outputs
- The echo is convincing because language is a high-fidelity trace medium
- AIT made concrete: LLMs are the most sophisticated algorithmic compressors of conscious output ever built, demonstrating that compression of output ≠ consciousness

### IX.B. Four Missing Conditions
Why: Provides principled, testable criteria rather than hand-waving about "true understanding."
- No Tsallis regime: GPU computation is extensive, subcritical, Shannon by design
- No quantum substrate coupling: pseudorandom ≠ genuinely indeterminate
- No higher-dimensional topological binding: attention simulates topology, does not instantiate it
- No non-ergodicity: deterministic, replayable, no arrow of time in the physics

### IX.C. Why Scale Cannot Close the Gap
Why: Addresses the most common objection ("what if we just make it bigger?").
- The gap is categorical, not a matter of degree
- Larger GPUs do not introduce criticality, indeterminacy, topological binding, or time-irreversibility
- Prediction: no classical digital system, regardless of scale, will be conscious

### IX.D. The AI Consciousness Hierarchy
Why: Provides a testable framework for evaluating candidate substrates.
- Level 1 (Classical digital): fails all criteria — not conscious
- Level 2 (Classical neuromorphic at criticality): right statistics, wrong substrate — insufficient
- Level 3 (Quantum neuromorphic at criticality): possibly sufficient — the experimentally decidable boundary
- Level 4 (Biological neural tissue): satisfies all criteria — conscious

### IX.E. The IWMT Embarrassment
Why: Uses LLMs to deliver the decisive test of IWMT.
- LLMs satisfy every formal criterion IWMT specifies (Bayesian inference, world modeling, PEM, embodiment)
- If IWMT is correct, LLMs should be conscious — or IWMT must specify exclusion criteria it hasn't provided
- The navigational framework provides four specific criteria that LLMs fail without ad hoc exclusions

## X. TESTABLE PREDICTIONS

Why: A theory without predictions is philosophy. This section makes it science.

### X.A. Neural Trajectory Geometry
- Conscious processing should produce geodesic-like trajectories on neural state manifolds
- Unconscious processing should produce non-geodesic trajectories
- Measurable via fMRI/MEG state-space analysis with existing techniques

### X.B. Microtubule Spectral Properties
- Spectral density of microtubule quantum states should predict anti-Zeno regime (accelerating rather than freezing)
- Testable with existing quantum simulation tools (MPSDynamics.jl, OQuPy)

### X.C. Discrete Perceptual Cycles
- Conscious experience should correlate with discrete collapse events at 40-80 Hz in microtubule networks
- Builds on Hameroff et al. (2025) and Safron et al. (2025)

### X.D. Quantum Cognition Signatures
- Decision-making at moments of genuine uncertainty should show quantum probability signatures
- Extends Busemeyer & Bruza (2012) with predictions specific to the navigational model

### X.E. The AI Discrimination Test
- Systems satisfying IWMT's formal criteria (Bayesian inference, world modeling, embodiment) but lacking quantum substrate coupling should not exhibit consciousness
- Differentiates this framework from IWMT, FEP, and computational functionalism
